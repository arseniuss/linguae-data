    # What is the Vocative Case?

    The vocative case is used to directly address someone or something. It is the case of direct address in Latin, meaning it is used when you call or speak to someone directly.

     - Example: “Marcus, come here!” or “Friend, listen!”

    In Latin, the vocative is used when a person or thing is being spoken to. It can be a name, a title, or a descriptive term.

    # Vocative Endings

    The vocative case usually has the same form as the nominative, but there are a few important rules to note:

     - Second Declension Masculine: For most second-declension masculine nouns ending in -us, the vocative case changes the -us ending to -e.
         - Example: Marcus (nominative) → Marce (vocative)
     - Other Declensions: In most other declensions (first, third, etc.), the vocative case is the same as the nominative.

    # Examples of the Vocative Case in Sentences

    Here are some examples using the vocative case:

     - *Marce, veni!* – Marcus, come!
     - *Puella, audi!* – Girl, listen!
     - *Amīce, salve!* – Friend, hello!

    # Using the Vocative with Imperatives

    The vocative case is often used with imperative verbs, which are commands or requests. For example:

     - *Marce, veni!* – Marcus, come!
     - *Amīce, audi!* – Friend, listen!

    In both cases, the vocative indicates the person being addressed, while the imperative is the action being requested.

    # Quid est Casus Vocativus?

    Casus vocativus adhibetur ad directam adlocutionem alicuius aut alicuius rei. Est casus directae adlocutionis in Latina, id est, adhibetur cum aliquem directe vocas aut alloqueris.

     - Exemplum: “Marce, veni huc!” vel “Amīce, audi!”

    In Latina, vocativus adhibetur cum persona aut res loquitur. Potest esse nomen, titulus, vel descriptio.

    # Fines Vocativi

    Casus vocativus plerumque simile est cum nominativo, sed sunt nonnullae regulæ notandae:

     - Secunda Declinatione Masculina: Pro plerisque substantivis masculinis secundae declinationis quae in -us finiunt, vocativus -us ad -e mutatur.
         - Exemplum: Marcus (nominativus) → Marce (vocativus)
     - Aliae Declinationes: In pluribus aliis declinationibus (prima, tertia, etc.), vocativus idem est ac nominativus.

    # Exempla Casus Vocativi in Sententiis

    Hic sunt quaedam exempla casus vocativi:

     - Marce, veni!
     - Puella, audi!
     - Amīce, salve!

    # Usus Vocativi cum Imperativis

    Casus vocativus saepe adhibetur cum verbis imperativi, quae sunt imperium aut petitio. Exempli gratia:

     - Marce, veni!
     - Amīce, audi!

    In utrisque casibus, vocativus indicat personam adlocutam, dum imperativus est actio quae petit.
