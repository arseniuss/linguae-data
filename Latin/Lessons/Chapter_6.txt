	## 1. What is the Vocative Case?

	The vocative case is used to directly address someone or something. It is
	the case of direct address in Latin, meaning it is used when you call or
	speak to someone directly.

	Example: “Marcus, come here!” or “Friend, listen!”

	In Latin, the vocative is used when a person or thing is being spoken to.
	It can be a name, a title, or a descriptive term.

	## 2. Vocative Endings

	The vocative case usually has the same form as the nominative, but there
	are a few important rules to note:

	 - Second Declension Masculine: For most second-declension masculine nouns
		ending in _-us_, the vocative case changes the _-us_ ending to _-e_.
	
	Example: _Marcus_ (nominative) → _Marce_ (vocative)

	 - Other Declensions: In most other declensions (first, third, etc.), the
	vocative case is the same as the nominative.

	## 3. Examples of the Vocative Case in Sentences

	Here are some examples using the vocative case:

	 - _Marce, venī!_ – Marcus, come!
	 - _Puella, audī!_ – Girl, listen!
	 - _Amīce, salvē!_ – Friend, hello!

	## 4. Using the Vocative with Imperatives

	The vocative case is often used with imperative verbs, which are commands
	or requests. For example:

	 - _Marce, venī!_ – Marcus, come!
	 - _Amīce, audī!_ – Friend, listen!

	In both cases, the vocative indicates the person being addressed, while the
	imperative is the action being requested.

	## 1. Quid est cāsus vocātīvus?

	Cāsus vocātīvus adhibētur ad directam adlocūtiōnem alicuius aut alicuius
	reī. Est cāsus directae adlocūtiōnis in Latīnā, id est, adhibētur cum
	aliquem dīrecte vocās aut alloqueris.

	Exemplum: “Marce, venī hūc!” vel “Amīce, audī!”

	In Latīnā, vocātīvus adhibētur cum persōna aut rēs loquitur. Potest esse
	nōmen, titulus, vel dēscrīptiō.

	## 2. Fīnēs vocātīvī

	Cāsus vocātīvus plērumque similis est cum nōminātīvō, sed sunt nōnnullae
	rēgulae notandae:

	 - Secunda dēclīnātiō māsculīna: Pro plērisque substandīvīs māsculīnīs
		secundae dēclīnātiōnis quae in _-us_ fīniunt, vocātīvus _-us_ ad _-e_
		mūtātur.

	Exemplum: Marcus (nōminātīvus) → Marce (vocātīvus)

	 - Aliae Dēclīnātiōnēs: In plūribus aliīs dēclīnātiōnibus (prīma, tertia,
	etc.), vocātīvus idem est ac nōminātīvus.

	## 3. Exempla cāsūs vocātīvī in sententiīs

	Hic sunt quaedam exempla cāsūs vocātīvī:

	 - Marce, venī!
	 - Puella, audī!
	 - Amīce, salvē!

	## 4. Ūsus vocātīvī cum imperātīvīs

	Cāsus vocātīvus saepe adhibētur cum verbīs imperātīvīs, quae sunt imperium
	aut petītiō. Exemplī grātiā:

	 - Marce, venī!
	 - Amīce, audī!

	In utrīsque cāsibus, vocātīvus indicat persōnam adlocūtam, dum imperātīvus
	est āctiō quae petitur.
