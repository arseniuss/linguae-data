	## 1. What is the Accusative of Purpose?

	The Accusative of Purpose is used in Latin with the prepositions _ad_ and
	_in_ to indicate motion toward a goal or the purpose of an action. These
	constructions are particularly common with verbs of movement or intent.

	Examples:
	 - _Puella ad scholam it._ – The girl goes to school.
	 - _Intrant in hortum._ – They enter into the garden.

	## 2. The Role of Prepositions (_ad_ and _in_)

	_Ad_ + Accusative: Expresses purpose or motion toward a place or goal.

	Example:
	 - _Ad templum currunt._ – They run to the temple.
	 - _Ad bellum parāmus._ – We prepare for war.

	_In_ + Accusative: Indicates motion into or onto something.

	Example:
	 - _Intrat in vīllam._ – He/she enters into the house.
	 - _In silvam ambulāmus._ – We walk into the forest.

	## 3. Accusative Endings

	The accusative endings depend on the declension of the noun being used.
	Here is a brief overview:

	| Declension               | Singular | Plural |
	| ------------------------ | -------- | ------ |
	| 1st Declension           | _-am_    | _-ās_  |
	| 2nd Declension           | _-um_    | _-ōs_  |
	| 3rd Declension           | _-em_    | _-ēs_  |
	| Neuter (all declensions) | _-um_    | _-a_   |

	## 4. Examples of the Accusative of Purpose

	Here are more examples of how the accusative case is used with _ad_ and
	_in_:

	 - _Mīlitēs ad urbem festīnant._ – The soldiers hurry to the city.
	 - _Puer in hortum currit._ – The boy runs into the garden.
	 - _Magister ad scholam venit._ – The teacher comes to the school.
	 - _Agricolae in agrōs eunt._ – The farmers go into the fields.

	## 1. Quid est accūsātīvus rationis?

	Accūsātīvus rationis in lātiōne cum prēpositionibus _ad_ et _in_ adhibētur
	ad mōtiōnem ad aliquod fīnem aut ad causam actiōnis significandam. Hae
	constructionēs praesertim cum verbīs mōtiōnis aut intentiōnis
	frequentissimae sunt.

	Exempla:
	 - Puella ad scholam it.
	 - Intrant in hortum.

	## 2. Rōle prēpositionum (_ad_ et _in_)

	_Ad_ + accūsātīvus: Rationem aut mōtiōnem ad locum aut finem exprimit.

	Exemplum:
	 - Ad templum currunt.
	 - Ad bellum parāmus.

	_In_ + Accūsātīvus: Mōtiōnem in aut super aliquid significat.

	Exemplum:
	 - Intrat in vīllam.
	 - In silvam ambulāmus.

	## 3. Desinentiae accūsātīvi

	Desinentiae accūsātīvi secundum declensionem nōminis adhibiti variantur.
	Hic est index brevissimus:

	| Declēnsiō                    | Sīngulāris | Plūrālis |
	| ---------------------------- | ---------- | -------- |
	| Prima declēnsiō              | _-am_      | _-ās_    |
	| Secunda declēnsiō            | _-um_      | _-ōs_    |
	| Tertia declēnsiō             | _-em_      | _-ēs_    |
	| Neutrum (omnia declēnsiōnēs) | _-um_      | _-a_     |

	## 4. Exempla accūsātīvi rationis

	Hic sunt plūra exempla de modo accūsātīvo cum _ad_ et _in_:

	 - Mīlitēs ad urbem festīnant.
	 - Puer in hortum currit.
	 - Magister ad scholam venit.
	 - Agricolae in agrōs eunt.
