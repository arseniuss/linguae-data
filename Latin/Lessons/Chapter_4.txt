chapter l6-ch1 <EOL
	## 1. What is the Dative Case?

	The dative case is used to show the indirect object in a sentence, which is
	the person or thing receiving the action indirectly. It answers the
	question, "To whom?" or "For whom?"

	Example: “He gives a gift to the girl.”

	In Latin, the indirect object changes its ending to show the dative case.

	## 2. Dative Endings for the First and Second Declensions

	Here are the dative singular endings for the first and second declensions:

	 - First Declension: _-ae_ (e.g., _puellae_ – "to/for the girl")
	 - Second Declension: _-ō_ (e.g., _amīcō_ – "to/for the friend")

	## 3. Examples of the Dative Case in Sentences

	Here are some examples using the dative case as the indirect object:

	 - _Puellae dōnum dat_ – He/she gives a gift to the girl.
	 - _Servō cibum parat_ – He/she prepares food for the servant.
	 - _Amīcō fābulam nārrat_ – He/she tells a story to the friend.

	In each example, the noun in the dative case indicates who is receiving the
	action indirectly.

	## 4. Practice with Direct and Indirect Objects

	Practice creating sentences that include both a direct object (in the
	accusative case) and an indirect object (in the dative case). This helps
	you build more complete sentences in Latin.

	## 1. Quid est cāsus datīvus?

	Cāsus datīvus adhibētur ad obiectum indirectum ostendendum, id est, persōna
	vel rēs quae āctiōnem indirectē recipit. Respondet quaestiōnibus, “Cui?”
	vel “Prō quō?”

    Exemplum: “Dōnum puellae dat.”

	In Latīnā, obiectum indirectum fīnem mūtat ut cāsum datīvum indicet.

	## 2. Fīnēs datīvī prīmae et secundae dēclīnātiōnis

	Hic sunt fīnēs datīvī singulāris prīmae et secundae dēclīnātiōnis:

	 - Prīma Dēclīnātiō: _-ae_ (ex. puellae)
	 - Secunda Dēclīnātiō: _-ō_ (ex. amīcō)

	## 3. Exempla cāsūs datīvī in sententiīs

	Hic sunt quaedam exempla ubi cāsus datīvus ut obiectum indirectum adhibētur:

	 - Puellae dōnum dat
	 - Servō cibum parat
	 - Amīcō fābulam nārrat

	In singulīs exemplīs, nōmen in cāsū datīvō indicat cui indirectē āctiō
	praestātur.

	## 4. Exercitātiō cum obiectīs directīs et indirectīs

	Exerceāmus sententias creandās quae et obiectum dīrectum (cāsus
	accūsātīvus) et obiectum indirectum (cāsus datīvus) habeant. Hōc modō
	sententias plēnās in linguā Latīnā formāre discimus.
