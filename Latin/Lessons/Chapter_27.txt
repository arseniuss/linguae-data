# Cover temporal phrases: ablative of time when ("tertia hōrā" - at the third
# hour) and accusative of duration ("multōs annōs" - for many years).
# Use conjunctions like dum, postquam, antequam in time clauses.

# Dum (while) – Used for actions happening at the same time (e.g., "Dum venis,
# ego laboro" – While you come, I work).
# Antequam (before) – Used to introduce a time clause (e.g., "Antequam veniat"
# – Before he comes).
# Postquam (after) – Used to introduce a time clause (e.g., "Postquam venit" –
# After he comes).
