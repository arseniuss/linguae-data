	## 1. Second Declension Nouns

	In this chapter, we learn nouns in the second declension. These nouns
	usually end in -us (masculine) or -um (neuter) in the nominative case.

	Examples:

	 - _servus_ – slave, servant (masculine)
	 - _amīcus_ – friend (masculine)
	 - _bellum_ – war (neuter)

	Like first declension nouns, these nouns change their endings to show their
	role in the sentence:

	 - Nominative case: Used for the subject. Example: _amīcus_ (the friend).
	 - Accusative case: Used for the object. Example: _amīcum_ (the friend).

	## 2. Adjectives and Agreement

	An adjective describes a noun, like "good," "big," or "small." In Latin,
	adjectives must agree with the noun they describe in gender (masculine,
	feminine, or neuter), number (singular or plural), and case (nominative,
	accusative, etc.).

	Here are some basic adjectives:

	 - _bonus_, _bona_, _bonum_ – good
	 - _magnus_, _magna_, _magnum_ – big, great

	Examples of adjective-noun agreement:

	 - _amīcus bonus_ – the good friend (masculine)
	 - _bellum magnum_ – the great war (neuter)

	## 3. Building More Complex Sentences

	Now, we can build more descriptive sentences by combining nouns with
	adjectives:

	_Amīcus bonus servum videt_ – The good friend sees the servant.
	 - _Amīcus bonus_ (good friend) is the subject in nominative case.
     - _Servum_ (servant) is the object in accusative case.
     - _Videt_ means "sees."

	## 1. Secunda dēclīnātiō nōmina

	In hōc capitulō, discimus nōmina in secunda dēclīnātiōne. Haec nōmina
	saepe in -us (masculīnum) vel -um (neutrum) in cāsū nōminātīvō fīniunt.

	Exempla:

	 - _servus_ – masculīnum
	 - _amīcus_ – masculīnum
	 - _bellum_ – neutrum

	Sicut prīmā dēclīnātiōne, haec nōmina suffixa mutant ad suam partem in
	sententiā mōnstrandam:

	 - Cāsus nōminātīvus: Ūsus est prō subiectō. Exemplum: _amīcus_.
	 - Cāsus Accūsātīvus: Ūsus est prō obiectō. Exemplum: _amīcum_.

	## 2. Adiectīva et concordantia

	Adiectīvum dēscrībit nōmen, sicut "bonus," "magnus," aut "parvus." In
	Latīnē, adiectīva concordāre dēbent cum nōmine quod dēscrībunt in genere
	(masculīnum, fēminīnum, neutrum), numerō (singulārī aut plūrālī), et cāsū
	(nōminātīvō, accūsātīvō, etc.).

	Hīc sunt quaedam adiectīva:

	 - _bonus_, _bona_, _bonum_
	 - _magnus_, _magna_, _magnum_

	Exempla concordantiae adiectīvōrum cum nōminibus:

	 - _amīcus bonus_ – masculīnum
	 - _bellum magnum_ – neutrum

	## 3. Sententiae māgis compositae

	Nunc, possumus sententias dēscriptīvīōrēs aedificāre nōmina et adiectīva
	coniungendō:

	_Amīcus bonus servum videt_

	 - _Amīcus bonus_ est subiectum in cāsū nōminātīvō.
	 - _Servum_ est obiectum in cāsū accūsātīvō.
