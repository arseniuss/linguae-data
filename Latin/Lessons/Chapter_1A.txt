chapter l1-ch1 <EOL

1. Quid est nōmen?

Nōmen est verbum prō persōnā, locō, rē, vel ideā, sīcut "puella", "aqua" aut "terra". In linguā Latīnā nōmina diversa suffīxa habent ad suam partem in sententiā mōnstrandam. Haec mūtātiō dēclīnātiō vocātur.
        
2. Prīma dēclīnātiō nōmina

Multa nōmina Latīna quae in -a fīniunt, in prīmā dēclīnātiōne sunt et saepe fēminīna sunt. Hic sunt aliqua exempla:

 - puella
 - aqua
 - terra

Haec nōmina suffixa mūtant secundum suum ūsum:

 - Cāsus nōminātīvus: Ūsus est prō subiectō (quod agere facit). Exemplum: puella.
 - Cāsus accūsātīvus: Ūsus est prō obiectō (quod āctiōnem accipit). Exemplum: puellam.

3. Sententiae simplicēs

Sententia Latīna simplex habet subiectum (persōnam vel rem quae āctiōnem facit) et verbum. Exemplī grātiā:

Puella aquam portat. 

 - Puella est subiectum in cāsū nōminātīvō.
 - Aquam est obiectum in cāsū accūsātīvō.

In linguā Latīnā ordo verbōrum variārī potest quia suffīxa nōbīs sēnsum ostendunt. Hoc nōs adiuvat ad intellectum suffīxōrum.


EOL <EOL
        1. What is a Noun?

        A noun is a word for a person, place, thing, or idea, like "girl," "water," or "land." In Latin, nouns have different endings to show their role in a sentence. This change is called declension.

        2. First Declension Nouns

        Many Latin nouns that end in -a are in the first declension and are usually feminine. Here are some examples:

         - puella – girl
         - aqua – water
         - terra – land

        These nouns change their endings depending on how they are used:

         - Nominative case: Used for the subject (the one doing the action). Example: puella (the girl).
         - Accusative case: Used for the object (the one receiving the action). Example: puellam (the girl).
        
        3. Simple Sentences

        A basic Latin sentence has a subject (the person or thing doing the action) and a verb. For example:

        Puella aquam portat – The girl carries water.
                 - Puella (the girl) is the subject in nominative case.
                 - Aquam (water) is the object in accusative case.
                 - Portat means "carries."

In Latin, word order is flexible because the endings tell us the meaning. This lets us focus on understanding how endings work.
