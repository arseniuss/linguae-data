chapter l1-ch1 <EOL
	## 1. What is a Noun?

	A noun is a word for a person, place, thing, or idea, like "girl",
	"water" or "land." In Latin, nouns have different endings to show their
	role in a sentence. This change is called declension.

	## 2. First Declension Nouns

	Many Latin nouns that end in -a are in the first declension and are
	usually feminine. Here are some examples:

	 - _puella_ – girl
	 - _aqua_ – water
	 - _terra_ – land

	These nouns change their endings depending on how they are used:

	 - Nominative case: Used for the subject (the one doing the action).
		Example: _puella_.
	 - Accusative case: Used for the object (the one receiving the action).
		Example: _puellam_.
	
	## 3. Simple Sentences

	A basic Latin sentence has a subject (the person or thing doing the
	action) and a verb. For example:

	_Puella aquam portat._ – The girl carries water.
	 - _Puella_ (the girl) is the subject in nominative case.
	 - _Aquam_ (water) is the object in accusative case.
	 - _Portat_ means "carries."

	In Latin, word order is flexible because the endings tell us the
	meaning. This lets us focus on understanding how endings work.
EOL <EOL

	## 1. Quid est nōmen?

	Nōmen est verbum prō persōnā, locō, rē, vel ideā, sīcut "puella", "aqua"
	aut "terra". In linguā Latīnā nōmina diversa suffīxa habent ad suam
	partem in sententiā mōnstrandam. Haec mūtātiō dēclīnātiō vocātur.
	
	## 2. Prīma dēclīnātiō nōmina

	Multa nōmina Latīna quae in -a fīniunt, in prīmā dēclīnātiōne sunt et
	saepe fēminīna sunt. Hic sunt aliqua exempla:

	 - _puella_
	 - _aqua_
	 - _terra_

	Haec nōmina suffixa mūtant secundum suum ūsum:

	 - Cāsus nōminātīvus: Ūsus est prō subiectō (quod agere facit).
		Exemplum: _puella_.
	 - Cāsus accūsātīvus: Ūsus est prō obiectō (quod āctiōnem accipit).
		Exemplum: _puellam_.

	## 3. Sententiae simplicēs

	Sententia Latīna simplex habet subiectum (persōnam vel rem quae
	āctiōnem facit) et verbum. Exemplī grātiā:

	_Puella aquam portat._
	 - _Puella_ est subiectum in cāsū nōminātīvō.
	 - _Aquam_ est obiectum in cāsū accūsātīvō.

	In linguā Latīnā ordo verbōrum variārī potest quia suffīxa nōbīs
	sēnsum ostendunt. Hoc nōs adiuvat ad intellectum suffīxōrum.
EOL
