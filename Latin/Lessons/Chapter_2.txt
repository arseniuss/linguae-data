	## 1. What is a Verb?

	A verb is a word that describes an action or state of being, like "to love",
	"to carry", or "to see". Verbs are essential in Latin sentences because
	they tell us what is happening and who is doing it.

	## 2. Conjugation in Latin

	Latin verbs change their endings depending on the subject (the person or
	thing doing the action). This change in endings is called conjugation. Each
	subject (I, you, he/she/it, we, you all, they) has its own ending in the
	present tense, which we’ll learn here.

	## 3. The Present Tense

	Present tense describes actions happening now.

	We’ll start with verbs in the first conjugation. First conjugation verbs
	have infinitives that end in _-āre_, like _amāre_ (to love) and _portāre_
	(to carry).

	## 4. Conjugating First Conjugation Verbs in the Present Tense

	Here are the endings for first conjugation verbs in the present tense.
	Each ending matches a different subject:

	| Subject        | Ending  | Latin    | English         |
	| -------------- | ------- | -------- | --------------- |
	| I              | _-ō_    | _amō_    | I love          |
	| You (singular) | _-ās_   | _amās_   | you love        |
	| He/She/It      | _-at_   | _amat_   | he/she/it loves |
	| We             | _-āmus_ | _amāmus_ | we love         |
	| You (plural)   | _-ātis_ | _amātis_ | you all love    |
	| They           | _-ant_  | _amant_  | they love       |

	Example with portare (to carry):

	 - _portō_ – I carry
	 - _portās_ – you carry
	 - _portat_ – he/she/it carries
	 - _portāmus_ – we carry
	 - _portātis_ – you all carry
	 - _portant_ – they carry

	## 5. Practice Sentences with Verbs

	Now that we know how to conjugate verbs, we can use them in simple
	sentences.

	 - _Egō amō_ – I love.
	 - _Tū portās aquam_ – You carry the water.
	 - _Puellam amat_ – He/she loves the girl.
	 - _Nōs laborāmus_ – We work.

	In Latin, the subject pronoun (like _egō_ or _tū_) is often optional
	because the verb ending already tells us the subject. So, _amō_ can mean
	"I love" without needing _egō_.

	## 6. Summary

	 - Verbs describe actions and change endings based on the subject.
	 - The present tense is used for actions happening now.
	 - First conjugation verbs end in _-āre_ and have specific endings for
		each subject (_-ō_, _-ās_, _-at_, _-āmus_, _-ātis_, _-ant_).
	 - Practice using verbs in sentences to get comfortable with these endings.

	## 1. Quid est verbum?

	Verbum est verbum quod āctiōnem vel statum dēscrībit, sīcut "amāre",
	"portāre" aut "vidēre". Verba sunt necessāria in sententiīs Latīnīs quia
	dīcunt quid fit et quis id facit.

	## 2. Coniugātiō in linguā latīnā

	Verba Latīna mutantur secundum subiectum (persōna vel rēs quae āctiōnem
	agit). Haec mūtātiō verbī fīnis dīcitur coniugātiō. Quaeque persōna (egō,
	tū, is/ea/id, nōs, vōs, eī) suum fīnem habet in tempore praesentī.

	## 3. Tempus praesēns

	Tempus praesēns dēscrībit āctiōnēs quae nunc fiunt.

	Incipiēmus cum verbīs in prīmā coniugātiōne. Prima coniugātiō habet verba
	quae in -āre in īnfīnītīvō finiunt, sīcut amāre et portāre.

	## 4. Coniugātiō verbōrum prīmae coniugātiōnis in tempore praesentī

	Hic sunt fīnēs prīmae coniugātiōnis verbōrum in tempore praesentī. Quaeque
	fīnis aliā persōnā apta est:

	| Persōna   | Fīnis latīnus | Exemplum |
	| --------- | ------------- | -------- |
	| Egō       | -ō            | amō      |
	| Tū        | -ās           | amās     |
	| Is/Ea/Id  | -at           | amat     |
	| Nōs       | -āmus         | amāmus   |
	| Vōs       | -ātis         | amātis   |
	| Eī/Eae/Ea | -ant          | amant    |

	Exemplum cum portāre

     - portō
     - portās
     - portat
     - portāmus
     - portātis
     - portant

	## 5. Exercitātiō cum sententiīs

	Nunc, cum scīmus verba coniugāre, ea in sententiīs simplicibus ūtī possumus.

     -Egō amō.
     - Tū portās aquam.
     - Puellam amat.
     - Nōs labōrāmus.

	In Latīnā, prōnōmen subiectīvum (sīcut egō aut tū) saepe nōn necessārium
	est quia fīnis verbī iam dēmonstrat subiectum. Itaque "amō" significāre
	potest "egō amō" sine "egō."

	## 6. Summārium

	 - Verba āctiōnēs dēscrībunt et fīnēs mutantur secundum subiectum.
     - Tempus praesēns est prō āctiōnibus quae nunc fiunt.
     - Verba prīmae coniugātiōnis in -āre finiunt et certōs fīnēs habent prō
		persōnā (-ō, -ās, -at, -āmus, -ātis, -ant).
     - Exercitātiō cum sententiīs adiuvat ad hās fīnēs intellegendās.
