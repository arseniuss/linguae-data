	## 1. What are Pronouns?

	A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun. In English, we use pronouns
	like "I," "you," "he," "she," and "it." Pronouns help us avoid repeating
	nouns.

	## 2. Personal Pronouns in Latin

	In Latin, pronouns also change their endings based on their role in the
	sentence (subject, object, etc.). Here are the basic singular pronouns:

	 - _ego_ – I (nominative, subject form)
	 - _mei_ – of me (genitive, possessive form)
	 - _tu_ – you (nominative, subject form)
	 - _tui_ – of you (genitive, possessive form)
	 - _is_, _ea_, _id_ – he, she, it (nominative, subject form)

	These pronouns allow us to create simple sentences without using specific
	names.

	## 3. Combining Nouns and Pronouns in Sentences

	Now that we know some pronouns, we can combine them with nouns and verbs to
	make different types of sentences:

    _Ego amīcus sum_ – I am a friend.

	 - _Ego_ (I) is the subject pronoun in nominative case.
	 - _Amīcus_ (friend) describes the subject.
	 - _Sum_ means "I am."

	_Tu puellam amas_ – You love the girl.

	 - _Tu_ (you) is the subject pronoun in nominative case.
	 - _Puellam_ (the girl) is the object in accusative case.
	 - _Amas_ means "you love."

	## 4. Practice Using Pronouns in Sentences

	Pronouns give us more options for sentence variety. Practicing with simple
	sentences helps us become comfortable with Latin word order and cases.

	## 1. Quid sunt prōnōmina?

	Prōnōmen est verbum quod prō nōmine pōnitur. In Anglicā, ūtīmur
	prōnōminibus sicut "I," "you," "he," "she," and "it." Prōnōmina nōs
	adiuvant nē nōmina repetāmus.

	## 2. In Latīnā, prōnōmina etiam suffixa mutant secundum suam partem in
	sententiā (subiectum, obiectum, etc.). Hīc sunt prōnōmina persōnālia
	singulāria:

	 - _ego_ –  nōminātīvus, forma subiectīva
	 - _meī_ – genitīvus, forma possessīva
	 - tū – nōminātīvus, forma subiectīva
	 - tuī – genitīvus, forma possessīva
	 - _is_, _ea_, _id_ – nōminātīvus, forma subiectīva

	Haec prōnōmina nōbīs permittunt ut simplicēs sententias sine speciāficīs
	nōminibus creēmus.

	## 3. Nōminibus et prōnōminibus in sententiīs coniungendīs

	Cum prōnōminibus nunc cognitīs, ea cum nōminibus et verbīs coniungere
	possumus ut variās sententias faciāmus:

	_Ego amīcus sum_

	 - _Ego_ est prōnōmen subiectīvum in cāsū nōminātīvō.
	 - _Amīcus_ dēscrībit subiectum.
	 - _Sum_ significat "ego sum."

	_Tū puellam amās._

	 - _Tū_ est prōnōmen subiectīvum in cāsū nōminātīvō.
	 - _Puellam_ est obiectum in cāsū accūsātīvō.

	## 4. Exercitātiō prōnōminibus in sententiīs ūtendīs

	Prōnōmina nōbīs plūrēs optiōnēs prō varietāte sententiarum dant.
	Exercitātiō cum simplicibus sententiīs nōs adiuvat ad ōrdinem verbōrum
	Latīnōrum et cāsūs intellegendōs.
