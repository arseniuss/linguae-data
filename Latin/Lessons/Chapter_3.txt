chapter l5-ch1 <EOL
	## 1. What is the Genitive Case?

	The genitive case is used to show possession or a relationship of belonging
	between two nouns. It answers the question, "Whose?" in English and is
	similar to using "’s" or "of" to show ownership.

	Example: "The farmer’s land" or "The land of the farmer."

	In Latin, the noun that shows possession changes its ending to indicate the
	genitive case.

    ## 2. Genitive Endings for the First and Second Declensions

	To use the genitive case, we need to know the correct endings. Here are the
	genitive singular endings for the first and second declensions:

	 - First Declension: _-ae_ (e.g., _puellae_ – "of the girl" or "the girl’s")
	 - Second Declension: _-ī_ (e.g., _amīcī_ – "of the friend" or "the
		friend’s")

	## 3. Examples of the Genitive Case in Sentences

	Here are some examples of using the genitive case to show possession:

     - _Agricolae vīlla_ – the farmer’s house / the house of the farmer
     - _Equī puerī_ – the boy’s horse / the horse of the boy
     - _Puellae liber_ – the girl’s book / the book of the girl

    In each example, the noun in the genitive case shows who owns or is
	associated with something else.

    ## 4. Practice with the Genitive Case

    To get used to using the genitive case, practice forming sentences like
	those above, showing who owns or is connected to various nouns.

	## 1. Quid est cāsus genetīvus?

	Cāsus genetīvus adhibētur ad possessiōnem vel relātiōnem inter duōs nōmina
	ostendendam. Respondet quaestiōnī "Cuius?" et similis est ūsū "’s" vel "of"
	in Anglicā linguā ad possessiōnem significandam.

	Exemplum: "Terra agricolae".

	In linguā Latīnā, nōmen quod possessiōnem ostendit suum fīnem mūtat ut
	cāsum genetīvum indicet.

	## 2. Fīnēs genetīvī prīmae et secundae dēclīnātiōnis

	Ad cāsum genetīvum rēctē adhibendum, fīnēs scīre dēbēmus. Hic sunt fīnēs
	genetīvī singulāris prīmae et secundae dēclīnātiōnis:

	 - Prīma Dēclīnātiō: _-ae_ (ex. puellae)
	 - Secunda Dēclīnātiō: -ī (ex. amīcī)

	## 3. Exempla cāsūs genetīvī in sententiīs

	Hic sunt quaedam exempla ad cāsum genetīvum adhibendum ad possessiōnem
	ostendendam:

	 - Agricolae vīlla
	 - Equī puerī
	 - Puellae liber

	In singulīs exemplīs, nōmen in cāsū genetīvō ostendit cui rēs aliae sunt
	vel cum quō coniunguntur.

	## 4. Exercitātiō cum cāsū genetīvō

	Ad cāsum genetīvum melius intellegendum, exerceāmus sententiās formandās ut
	possessiōnem in linguā Latīnā ostendere discāmus.
