    # What is the Genitive Case?

    The genitive case is used to show possession or a relationship of belonging between two nouns. It answers the question, "Whose?" in English and is similar to using "’s" or "of" to show ownership.

    Example: "The farmer’s land" or "The land of the farmer."

    In Latin, the noun that shows possession changes its ending to indicate the genitive case.

    # Genitive Endings for the First and Second Declensions

    To use the genitive case, we need to know the correct endings. Here are the genitive singular endings for the first and second declensions:

     - First Declension: -ae (e.g., puellae – "of the girl" or "the girl’s")
     - Second Declension: -i (e.g., amici – "of the friend" or "the friend’s")

    # Examples of the Genitive Case in Sentences

    Here are some examples of using the genitive case to show possession:

     - Agricolae villa – the farmer’s house / the house of the farmer
     - Equi pueri – the boy’s horse / the horse of the boy
     - Puellae liber – the girl’s book / the book of the girl

    In each example, the noun in the genitive case shows who owns or is associated with something else.

    # Practice with the Genitive Case

    To get used to using the genitive case, practice forming sentences like those above, showing who owns or is connected to various nouns.

    # Quid est Casus Genetivus?

    Casus genetivus adhibetur ad possessionem vel relationem inter duos nomina ostendendam. Respondet quaestioni "Cuius?" et similis est usui "’s" vel "of" in Anglica lingua ad possessionem significandam.

    Exemplum: "Terra agricolae" sive "The farmer’s land."

    In lingua Latina, nomen quod possessionem ostendit suum finem mutat ut casum genetivum indicet.

    # Fines Genetivi Primae et Secundae Declinationis

    Ad casum genetivum recte adhibendum, fines scire debemus. Hic sunt fines genetivi singularis primae et secundae declinationis:

     - Prima Declinatione: -ae (ex. puellae – "of the girl" sive "puellae")
     - Secunda Declinatione: -i (ex. amici – "of the friend" sive "amici")

    # Exempla Casus Genetivi in Sententiis

    Hic sunt quaedam exempla ad casum genetivum adhibendum ad possessionem ostendendam:

     - Agricolae villa
     - Equi pueri
     - Puellae liber

    In singulis exemplis, nomen in casu genetivo ostendit cui res aliae sunt vel cum quo coniunguntur.

    # Exercitatio cum Casu Genetivo

    Ad casum genetivum melius intellegendum, exerceamus sententias formandas ut possessionem in lingua Latina ostendere discamus.
