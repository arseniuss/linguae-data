        1. Quid est Nomen?

        Nomen est verbum pro persona, loco, re, vel idea, sicut "puella," "aqua," aut "terra." In Latine, nomina diversa suffixa habent ad suam partem in sententia monstrandam. Haec mutatio declinatio vocatur.
        
        2. Prima Declinatio Nomina

        Multa nomina Latina quae in -a finiunt in prima declinatione sunt et saepe femina sunt. Hic sunt aliqua exempla:

         - puella
         - aqua
         - terra

        Haec nomina suffixa mutant secundum suam usum:
         - Casus Nominativus: Usus est pro subiecto (qui agere facit). Exemplum: puella (puella).
         - Casus Accusativus: Usus est pro obiecto (qui actionem accipit). Exemplum: puellam (puella).

        3. Sententiae Simplices

        Sententia Latina simplex habet subiectum (persona vel res quae actionem facit) et verbum. Exempli gratia:

        Puella aquam portat
         - Puella est subiectum in casu nominativo.
         - Aquam est obiectum in casu accusativo.

        In Latina, ordo verborum variari potest quia suffixa nobis sensum ostendunt. Hoc nos adiuvat ad intellectum suffixorum.

        1. What is a Noun?

        A noun is a word for a person, place, thing, or idea, like "girl," "water," or "land." In Latin, nouns have different endings to show their role in a sentence. This change is called declension.

        2. First Declension Nouns

        Many Latin nouns that end in -a are in the first declension and are usually feminine. Here are some examples:

         - puella – girl
         - aqua – water
         - terra – land

        These nouns change their endings depending on how they are used:

         - Nominative case: Used for the subject (the one doing the action). Example: puella (the girl).
         - Accusative case: Used for the object (the one receiving the action). Example: puellam (the girl).
        
        3. Simple Sentences

        A basic Latin sentence has a subject (the person or thing doing the action) and a verb. For example:

        Puella aquam portat – The girl carries water.
                 - Puella (the girl) is the subject in nominative case.
                 - Aquam (water) is the object in accusative case.
                 - Portat means "carries."
        In Latin, word order is flexible because the endings tell us the meaning. This lets us focus on understanding how endings work.
